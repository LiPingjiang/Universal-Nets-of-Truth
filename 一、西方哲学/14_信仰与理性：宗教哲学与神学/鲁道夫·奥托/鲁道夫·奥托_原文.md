# 鲁道夫·奥托原文选读

## 甲译·白话意译

### 选段一：《神圣者》——不可言说的震颤

试着回想你人生中某个让你感到毛骨悚然却又被深深吸引的时刻——不是因为危险，不是因为美，而是某种说不清道不明的东西，某种……**完全他者**。

这就是宗教体验的核心：**畏惧的神秘**（mysterium tremendum et fascinans）。它是"神秘的"——因为它超越一切可以把握的概念；它是"令人战栗的"（tremendum）——因为它带来一种独特的敬畏恐惧，不同于任何日常恐惧；它是"令人着迷的"（fascinans）——因为它同时以不可抗拒的力量吸引着你。

这种体验，奥托称之为"神圣感"（das Heilige，the Holy）。**它不能被完全理性化，不能被完全道德化，它有独特的、不可化约的内容。**

### 选段二：《神圣者》——努米诺斯体验

**"努米诺斯"（das Numinose）**是奥托造的新词，指宗教体验中那个无法被还原为其他任何东西的特殊质量。

它不是对强大力量的恐惧（那是世俗意义上的恐惧），也不是对善的崇拜（那是道德），也不是对美的赞叹（那是审美）——它是某种独特的、只在宗教体验中出现的东西：**在绝对他者面前的无化感与着迷感的结合**。

努米诺斯体验有以下特征：
1. **神秘（mysterium）**：完全他者，超越一切概念把握
2. **战栗（tremendum）**：独特的宗教恐惧，包含威严、压倒性力量、"绝对"的维度
3. **着迷（fascinans）**：同时的强烈吸引，神圣的美丽与恩宠

### 选段三：《东西方神秘主义》——神圣体验的跨文化共性

无论是基督教神秘主义（梅斯特·艾克哈特）、印度吠檀多、伊斯兰苏菲主义，还是东方禅，它们各自表达着同一个核心：**对神圣他者的直接体验**，那个超越日常经验的维度。

宗教的理性部分——教义、道德律、神学系统——是对这个核心体验的后续概念化。**但体验本身先于概念，且永远无法被概念完全穷尽。**宗教研究应当从这个核心体验出发，而非从外在的社会功能或道德规范出发。

## 乙译·学术直译

### Text 1: *The Idea of the Holy* — The Numinous

**The feeling of it may at times come sweeping like a gentle tide, pervading the mind with a tranquil mood of deepest worship. It may pass over into a more set and lasting attitude of the soul, continuing, as it were, thrillingly vibrant and resonant... It has its wild and demonic forms and can sink to an almost grisly horror and shuddering. It has its crude, barbaric antecedents and early manifestations, and again it may be developed into something beautiful and pure and glorious. It may become the hushed, trembling, and speechless humility of the creature in the presence of—whom or what? In the presence and sight of that which is a Mystery inexpressible and above all creatures.**

### Text 2: *The Idea of the Holy* — Mysterium Tremendum

**The 'tremendum' has... the note of 'dread'; but 'dread' here is taken in its deepest and most original sense, not in the sense of mere fear... The 'tremendum' has... a unique quality, to which we can do no more justice... than by calling it 'dread' with a qualifying adjective 'awe.'** For 'awe' is itself merely an approximation, conveying, it is true, something of the 'tremendum' in its softer forms, but not the profounder 'tremendum' of the numinous experience itself.

The 'fascinans' is the positive quality of the numinous, its strange attractiveness, the lure of the 'entirely other': **'the irrational and ineffable content'—content not conceivable in rational concepts—which is the inmost kernel of all religion.**

### Text 3: *Mysticism East and West*

**In Indian and in Christian mysticism we find the same impulse—to break through the world of phenomena to some ultimate reality...** though the paths diverge widely in their conceptual expression, in their imagery, in the specific character of the Atman or the 'ground of the soul,' the structural parallel is unmistakable.
